My Tourist Transformation: A Kona Adventure

When We All Went Our Separate Ways

So there I was, fully embracing my inner tourist while the guys headed off to check out the Green Sand Beach near South Point. We all had different ideas for how to spend the day – they wanted beaches, I wanted to explore downtown Kona!

Early Bird Gets the Island Creation

Being the early riser that I am, I found myself wandering down Ali’i Drive – basically downtown Kona’s main drag – and decided to fuel up at the Fish Hopper Restaurant. Nothing says “island life” quite like enjoying an “island creation” breakfast, like eggs and Portuguese sausage, in their open-air dining area while watching the locals casually hop into the ocean for their morning swim. There’s something magical about waterfront dining when you’ve actually been awake to see the sunrise.

The Great Souvenir Safari (With Photo Ops Galore)

After breakfast, I embarked on what can only be described as an epic quest through every single alley and shop in the area. And when I say I browsed through souvenirs, I mean I browsed through souvenirs – like, ALL of them. T-shirts with questionable slogans? Check. Miniature ukuleles that definitely won’t survive the flight home? Double check.

But here’s where it gets good – my wandering adventure turned into an impromptu photo shoot! Around every corner was another picture-perfect moment just begging to be captured.

From old church buildings to Art Gallery masterpieces, Kona was basically serving up Instagram content on a silver platter.

My New Furry (and Feathered?) Crew

The best part? I somehow managed to make friends with all sorts of adorable animals along the way, and yes, they absolutely made it into the photo collection.

There’s something about wandering around downtown Kona that just leads to the most unexpected animal encounters. Who knew being a walking tourist stereotype would come with its own little petting zoo experience AND a built-in photography portfolio?​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​

Time to Refuel: Beat the Heat Hawaiian Style

It’s hot and all of that wandering has worked up an appetite so it’s time to cool off at Humpty‘s with some calamari and a Longboard Lager from Kona Brewing.

Today is brought to you by the letter B.

A Perfect Day in Paradise: The Five B’s of Kona

Breakfast, Bikes, Beaches, Bros, Breeze, Blue water

Sometimes the most beautiful days are defined by simple pleasures, and today was a masterclass in the art of island living.

Breakfast: Fuel for Paradise

The morning began with the kind of breakfast that perfectly captures our island mindset—unpretentious yet satisfying. From our well-stocked Costco provisions, I crafted something unexpectedly delightful: a French toast bagel crowned with creamy brie cheese, paired with another cup of that rich Kona coffee that never fails to remind me why this place has captured my heart.

There’s something magical about starting your day with local flavors while watching the morning light dance across the Pacific. The coffee alone tells the story of this island—bold, smooth, and unforgettable.

Bros and Breakfast, Round Two

When the boys finally stirred, we ventured out for their morning fuel. While they tackled their breakfast, I indulged in a morning cocktail (11:00 Pacific)—because vacation time operates by different rules, and sometimes a little liquid sunshine is exactly what the moment calls for.

Bikes, Beaches, and Brotherly Adventures

Our next destination was the iconic Lava Lava Beach, but not before making an essential pitstop at the local Harley-Davidson dealership. You can’t visit paradise without bringing a piece of it home, and we picked up some perfect souvenirs for Mom—tokens of appreciation from her adventurous sons.

Beach Bliss at Lava Lava

Then came the main event: pure, unadulterated relaxation at Lava Lava Beach. Here, time moves differently. The sunshine painted everything golden, the sea breeze carried away every worry, and those impossibly blue waters reminded us why people dream of places like this.

We surrendered completely to the moment—no agenda, no schedule, just three guys soaking in the natural beauty that makes Kona feel like the edge of the world in the best possible way.

Beer and Sunset: The Perfect Finale


As the day wound down, we found ourselves back at Laverne’s with Chris and Art, cold beers in hand, watching another spectacular Kona sunset paint the sky in impossible colors. There’s a ritual to these evenings now—a sacred pause where we simply witness the day’s grand finale.


Each sunset here feels like nature showing off, and tonight was no exception. The sky blazed with oranges and pinks that would look fake in a photograph, while gentle waves provided the perfect soundtrack to end another unforgettable day in paradise.

In Kona, perfection isn’t about grand gestures—it’s about French toast bagels with brie, morning cocktails with your favorite people, and sunsets that remind you why some places become more than destinations. They become part of your soul.

From Seatbelt Shenanigans to Lava Letdowns

Yesterday's adventure began with what I thought would be a simple morning ritual—brewing a special blend of coffee that wasn't quite the tropical island vibe that you would expect for an island vibe, but it had this unmistakable Irish flair to it. As I stood there in the kitchen, inhaling the rich aroma and contemplating this curious choice-feinated epiphany: the Irish theme wasn't random at all, because Irish my family was with me for this adventure.

After our morning coffee, we decided to Uber to the airport for our rental car pickup—what could go wrong, right? Well, apparently everything when your driver speaks zero English and my friend Gary somehow managed to turn himself into a human seatbelt puzzle. Picture this: our Spanish-speaking driver is pulling away and Gary is still trapped in the backseat, frantically gesturing like he's trying to escape from Houdini's latest trick. We're all laughing hysterically on the sidewalk while Gary's getting an unexpected tour of the airport loop until our bewildered driver finally notices his accidental passenger and pulls over for an impromptu seatbelt tutorial. Once we finally liberated Gary and secured our rental car, we rallied the troops and headed across the island to catch some volcano action—because who doesn't want to see molten lava after hearing it was active the night before?

Mother Nature, however, had other plans and served us a big helping of disappointment instead of the fiery spectacle we'd hoped for.

Thankfully, Lanakai Brewery swooped in to save the day with craft beers and those incredible Ulu tacos (think plantain meets potato in the most delicious way possible), and we ended our adventure back in Kona where the only lava flow we witnessed was in a cocktail glass which, honestly, was exactly the kind of volcanic experience this day deserved.

A day unplanned

Yesterday unfolded like one of those perfect vacation days you never want to end—the kind that starts with zero plans and somehow becomes exactly what your soul needed.

I began poolside, letting the sun work its magic while I pretended to read but mostly just existed in that blissful state of doing absolutely nothing.

When restlessness finally kicked in, we ventured into town where I found myself drawn to the old Catholic Church, camera in hand, capturing its weathered beauty against the tropical sky.

Then the beach called me. I stood on the shore trying to get the perfect shot of waves exploding against the lava rocks, getting thoroughly soaked in the process but loving every second of it.

As evening approached, we landed at Huggos where friends materialized as they do in vacation towns, turning a casual drink into an impromptu reunion filled with laughter and stories. Dinner across the street at Laverne's stretched into hours of the kind of conversation that makes you forget to check your phone, and just when I thought the day couldn't get any better, Mother Nature served up another one of her show-stopping sunsets, painting the sky in impossible colors while we all stood there like tourists in our own lives, completely mesmerized.

We have Arrived 🌊🏝️

After what felt like an eternity—three grueling hours stuck in Portland's airport followed by five more hours crammed into an airplane seat—I finally stumbled off that plane onto the Big Island, and let me tell you, it was like stepping into paradise itself. The moment that warm Hawaiian air hit my face and the ocean breeze wrapped around me like a gentle hug, every single minute of travel hell instantly melted away, replaced by the intoxicating realization that I had officially escaped reality and landed in my own personal slice of heaven.

The clock struck five and suddenly I found myself swept up in the birthday brigade, heading to Sunset Kai where the happy hour promised more than just discounted drinks—it was Hien's big 6-0, and nothing was going to stop us from toasting this milestone in proper style. As we settled into our seats with the sun painting the sky in brilliant oranges and pinks, I couldn't help but marvel at how life's sweetest moments often come disguised as simple happy hour plans, watching my friend embrace this new decade with the same infectious energy that had carried him through the previous five.
